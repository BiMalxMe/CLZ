Nepali Football

Football in Nepal is more than just a sport—it’s a symbol of passion, unity, and pride. With a history dating back to the early 20th century, Nepali football has evolved despite challenges.

The All Nepal Football Association (ANFA) was established in 1951 and joined FIFA in 1970. Since then, Nepal has participated in multiple regional and international tournaments. The national team, known as the "Gorkhalis," won the SAFF Championship in 1993 and continues to strive for excellence.

Nepal’s Martyr’s Memorial A-Division League is the country’s top domestic league, featuring clubs like Manang Marshyangdi Club, Tribhuvan Army Club, and Machhindra FC. Players like Rohit Chand have gained international recognition by playing in foreign leagues, helping put Nepal on the global football map.

While challenges like infrastructure and funding persist, Nepali football is making steady progress. With passionate fans, rising talents, and increased exposure, the future of Nepali football is bright!
