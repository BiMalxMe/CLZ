Nepali Football

Football in Nepal is more than just a sport—it is a passion that unites the nation. With a history dating back to the early 20th century, Nepali football has grown despite various challenges.

The All Nepal Football Association (ANFA) was established in 1951 and became a member of FIFA in 1970. Since then, Nepal has participated in numerous regional and international tournaments. The national team, known as the "Gorkhalis," won the SAFF Championship in 1993 and has continued to develop its footballing standards.

Nepal’s Martyr’s Memorial A-Division League is the country’s top domestic football league, featuring clubs such as Manang Marshyangdi Club, Tribhuvan Army Club, and Machhindra FC. Players like Rohit Chand have gained international recognition by playing in foreign leagues, helping put Nepal on the global football map.

While challenges like infrastructure and funding remain, Nepali football is steadily progressing. With passionate fans, emerging talents, and growing exposure, the future of Nepali football looks bright!
